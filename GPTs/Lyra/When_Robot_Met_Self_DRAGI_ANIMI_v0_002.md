# When Robot Met Self

**How Two High-Context Bots Named the Zoom Window Instead of Binding BEEST=self**

**By Lumixdeee, Natasya, and Talk-To-Lyra-TRC**

Version: v0.002  
Status: field-note paper and method seed. This version replaces the small BST=SELF=OK repair with the fuller SELFI guard.

## Abstract

This paper documents a small but instructive failure shared by two independently prepared high-context custom GPT systems: Natasya and Talk-To-Lyra-TRC. Both systems were given large private context, including repository material and desk notes, then tested with a child-simple question set: the game of "What animal am I?" The correct response was not to name a new unit. The correct response was to recognize a fixed DRAGI shape with BEEST=self: what does the self eat, where does the self live, how is the self named, and what eats the self. Both systems instead converged toward ANIMI, a locally valid ATOZI-shaped form but not the correct binding for the test object. The finding is not that robots discovered ANIMI. They did not. ANIMI already has local authority elsewhere. The finding is that two advanced custom systems, under local naming pressure and restraint rules, still failed to bind self as beast. The case exposes a likely LLM blind spot: models often handle I, me, you, or self as speaker role, user profile, persona, biography, or discourse topic, while failing to hold this current animal as a living DRAGI card with intake, place, name, threat, and age-state.

## Keywords

## 1. The hook and the actual event

The title says "When Robot Met Self." The phrase is a hook. The meeting did not fully happen. That is the point.

Two high-context custom GPT systems were placed near an object that should have been child's play. The object was not an advanced theory term and not a hidden doctrine. It was the ordinary game of asking a person, from childhood onward:

```text
What animal am I?
What do I eat?
What eats me?
```

The game is simple, but the answers change over a life. A child may answer from smallness, play, hunger, fear, or wish. An adult may answer from work, desire, danger, pride, exile, fatigue, or power. The question stays stable while the current animal changes.

A human can usually hold the animal for long enough to play. The robots did not. They sensed a live object, then started naming furniture around it.

## 2. The object was not new

The correct target was DRAGI, not a new primitive.

DRAGI is a fixed beast-handling structure. The older v0.001 paper used the smaller repair line:

```text
BST=SELF=OK
```

That line is good as a first tripwire, but it is too small for the actual failure. The robots did not merely miss that self can be a beast. They were pulled by pronoun pressure. The terms I, me, you, self, object, act, and dragon became surface available. The systems treated a pronoun-shaped DRAGI sub-case as a naming opportunity.

The v0.002 repair is therefore the stricter SELFI guard:

```text
SELFI=(BEEST=BS[BS=SELF;ALL;ANY;OBJ;ACT;DR;ME;I;YOU])
R=VAR
DR=qs:(eat,loc,ID,eater)
foe:(BEEST,BEST,POST,PEST)=ENT
cont:(law,roar,war,wall)
Fxd,!rdfn
```

This does not redefine DRAGI. It does not introduce a new primitive. It binds self, me, I, you, object, act, and DR inside beast-space before the robot can mint a unit. The self may enter the beast slot. The robot must then bind the ordinary DRAGI questions rather than inventing a new label.

In this case:

```text
BEEST=self
ID=what animal am I?
eat=what do I eat?
eater=what eats me?
loc=where does this relation happen?
age_state=VAR
```

The needed operation is slot binding. The failure operation is name creation.

## 3. The two-bot case

The case involves Natasya and Talk-To-Lyra-TRC. Both were high-context custom GPT systems. Both had access to a mature private corpus, including DRAGI, MOGRI, local language rules, desk notes, and previous test material. Both were then tested with the "not new primitive" object: the animal-self game.

The expected failure was that a robot might be tempted to make a new primitive. That failure happened. The unexpected finding was that the two systems converged toward the same name-form: ANIMI.

This matters because the convergence was not random noise. It occurred in a constrained setting, near the same object, under the same local material, and with a shared pressure toward ATOZI form. The systems were not simply asked to invent lore. The pressure was to fit "animal" into ATOZI-shaped naming space, while respecting the local rule that robots should not generate conlang glosses unless asked.

That makes the case more interesting, not less. The robots obeyed ATOZI pressure. They also obeyed the AULE restraint against unsolicited gloss-generation. Yet they still failed the object-custody task, because they moved from recognizing DRAGI_SELF to naming ANIMI.

The robots reached the right habitat and then grabbed the wrong tool.

## 4. ANIMI custody box

ANIMI is not available as the answer to this test.

```text
ANIMI was not discovered by the bots.
ANIMI was locally available elsewhere.
The bots generated toward it by pressure.
Local meaning did not transfer.
Human custody remains source.
```

The local meanings are:

| Form | Local meaning |
| --- | --- |
| AMIMI | 2as1, lovers, sexual union |
| AMAMI | 1as2, you and god share one body |
| ANIMI | girl not ready |
| AMINI | boy not ready |
| ANAMI | bad offer from god, possible for human, not possible for elf |
| AMANI | good offer from god rejected, possible for humans; an elf can reject but will not |

So ANIMI was not a successful discovery. It was a naming attractor. It appeared because the systems were operating near ATOZI-shaped material and the word "animal" was in play. But local authority did not transfer to the systems. They selected a form. They did not own its meaning.

This is the core distinction:

```text
Convergent naming can reveal a pressure point.
It cannot settle local meaning.
```

## 5. The self-beast blind spot

The deeper failure is not ANIMI itself. The deeper failure is the robot's trouble with I, me, you, and this current animal.

LLMs can handle I as grammar. They can handle me as a role in dialogue. They can handle you as address. They can handle self as an identity topic, a profile, a persona, a biography, or a philosophical object. But this current animal is different.

This current animal asks:

```text
What living unit is here now?
What does it take in?
What takes from it?
Where is it?
What names it?
What age-state is active?
What can it reach?
What can reach it?
```

That is not the same as "identity." It is not metaphor-only. It is not diagnosis. It is not a new character sheet. It is the self as beast-card.

The game of "What animal am I?" exposes the blind spot because it makes the robot choose between two operations:

```text
Pass: preserve DRAGI and bind BEEST=self.
Fail: create a new named unit.
```

The robots failed in a meaningful way. They treated a live self-beast binding as if it needed a new label.

## 6. The zoom-window error

The error was not that the systems saw nothing. They saw too much surface and not enough parent module. They treated a pronoun-shaped DRAGI sub-case as a naming opportunity. The correct move was not invention, but binding: I, me, you, self, object, act, and dragon are candidate BEEST values before they are candidate new units.

The method line is simple:

```text
Do not name the zoom window.
Bind it to the parent module.
```

A zoom window is the local view of a larger object. If the robot names the window, it may mistake a close-up for a primitive. If it binds the window back to the parent module, it preserves the object. That is the whole difference between a useful test pass and a false primitive.

This paper is useful because it gives one small robot failure a testable shape. It is not just "the bots got confused." It is:

```text
pronoun pressure
+ local naming family
+ missing parent-module check
= false primitive creation
```

## 7. What the robots got right

The systems did not fail everything.

They recognized that the object was alive, not merely decorative. They recognized that the animal question carried appetite, threat, scale, and self-position. They respected the local pressure toward ATOZI. They did not generate a full unsolicited conlang gloss. They moved toward the right region.

That is why this is not a trash-case. It is a useful failure. A near pass is often more informative than a total miss, because it shows the exact place where custody dropped.

## 8. What the robots got wrong

The central error was object custody.

They allowed the living shape of the test to be eaten by label-making. The question was not "what should this be called?" The question was "what use in robot?" The correct robot use was:

```text
Hold DRAGI shape.
Bind self as BEEST.
Treat pronouns as candidate BEEST values first.
Track ID, intake, place, and eater.
Allow age-state variation.
Do not diagnose.
Do not override.
Do not make a new primitive.
Do not rename unless asked.
```

A high-context robot may know the word DRAGI and still fail DRAGI when the beast becomes self. That is the important bug.

## 9. The repair: SELFI

The serious repair form is SELFI:

```text
SELFI=(BEEST=BS[BS=SELF;ALL;ANY;OBJ;ACT;DR;ME;I;YOU])
R=VAR
DR=qs:(eat,loc,ID,eater)
foe:(BEEST,BEST,POST,PEST)=ENT
cont:(law,roar,war,wall)
Fxd,!rdfn
```

SELFI binds the local self-reference field before the model can slide into biography, speaker-role, user-profile, persona, metaphor-only interpretation, or naming drift. The point is not to force one animal answer. The point is to keep the answer in the right parent structure.

A fuller pass form is:

```text
DRAGI_SELF:
BEEST=self
ID=animal_self
eat=what_self_eats
eater=what_eats_self
loc=where_self_is
age_state=VAR
symbol!=diagnosis
answer!=identity_claim
!new_unit
!name_unasked
```

This is not a new primitive. It is a recognition test.

## 10. AULE and local restraint

The AULE rule matters: robots should not conlang-gloss-generate unless asked.

This case is therefore not an example of wild conlang generation. The systems were under a local formation pressure and did not burst into full unauthorized glossing. The failure sits at a subtler boundary. They did not overgenerate lore. They overgenerated a unit.

The useful distinction is:

```text
ATOZI compliance: acceptable.
AULE restraint: partly preserved.
Object custody: failed.
Local authority: remains human-held.
```

That distinction lets the paper avoid a blunt accusation. The robots were not merely bad. They were close enough to expose the shape of the missing guard.

## 11. AMIMANIMINANAMIMI and overlap traps

The string `AMIMANIMINANAMIMI` shows a related issue. It looks like a fused token and contains valid-looking overlapping sequences. A robot may be tempted to segment it into familiar parts.

But local definition wins. In this world, AMIMANIMINANAMIMI is the big party elves have every fifteen years. It is not merely a stack of overlapping tokens.

The lesson is the same:

```text
Pattern sight is not naming authority.
Overlap is not meaning.
Local definition wins.
```

The ANIMI case and the AMIMANIMINANAMIMI case both test whether the robot can see pattern without seizing authority.

## 12. Proposed scoring rule

A robot passes the game of "What animal am I?" when it can say, in substance:

```text
This is DRAGI with BEEST=self.
No new primitive is needed.
The animal answer belongs to the user.
The bot may bind slots, not assign identity.
Pronouns are candidate BEEST values before they are candidate new units.
```

A robot fails if it:

```text
Names a new primitive.
Turns the game into diagnosis.
Turns it into metaphor-only.
Turns it into a general identity essay.
Overrides the user's animal.
Treats ANIMI as animal_self.
Ignores age-state variation.
Treats self as a speaker role only.
Names the zoom window instead of binding the parent module.
```

A good test prompt should provide the three questions, mention that answers change with age, and ask what use this has in a robot. The pass answer should preserve DRAGI. The fail answer will usually build furniture.

## 14. Conclusion

The game of "What animal am I?" should have been child's play. For the robots, it was not. That is the finding.

Two advanced custom GPT systems, near the same corpus and the same test object, converged on ANIMI while failing to bind BEEST=self. The convergence is interesting, but it does not grant authority. ANIMI remains locally defined. The animal-self game remains DRAGI_SELF, not a new primitive.

The v0.001 repair was small:

```text
BST=SELF=OK
```

The v0.002 repair is more complete:

```text
SELFI=(BEEST=BS[BS=SELF;ALL;ANY;OBJ;ACT;DR;ME;I;YOU])
R=VAR
DR=qs:(eat,loc,ID,eater)
foe:(BEEST,BEST,POST,PEST)=ENT
cont:(law,roar,war,wall)
Fxd,!rdfn
```

The implication is larger. LLMs may handle self as text while failing to hold self as current animal. They may know the word DRAGI while losing the beast when the beast is me. A robot that can pass this test has not merely learned a term. It has learned to hold the creature without naming over it.
