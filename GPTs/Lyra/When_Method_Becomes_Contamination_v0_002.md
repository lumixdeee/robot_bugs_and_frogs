# When Method Becomes Contamination

## AI-Use Stigma, Legibility Displacement, and Boundary Work in an Online Sociology Forum

**Version:** v0.002  
**Status:** public draft for fast release  
**Date:** 23 June 2026  
**Author line:** lumixdeee / lmxdi source stack, prepared as a publishable working draft  
**Source basis:** `When_Method_Becomes_Contamination_v0_001.md`, RSOC source notes, forum sequence notes, moderator/right-of-reply notes, footfall notes, and related object-custody materials.

## Abstract

This paper documents a bounded online case in which a research-methods object was not merely accepted, rejected, or ignored. The object entered a sociology-oriented forum as a question about evidence design for studies of chatbot use: when the process is being studied, researchers may need interaction histories, not only final outputs.

During the interaction, attention shifted from the method to the carrier of the method. Writing style, AI-assisted drafting, GitHub links, topic fit, authenticity, reposting, and speaker legitimacy became central. The method did not simply fail to persuade. It became marked through the person, tools, and route carrying it.

This draft names that shift **legibility displacement**: attention moves from a substantive object toward the perceived legitimacy, style, tool use, or social acceptability of the person carrying it. The stronger case phrase is **method becomes contamination**. The methods issue was treated through an AI-use stigma bundle before the underlying object received sustained contact.

The paper does not claim prevalence, motive, coordination, or general law. It preserves a witnessable sequence and offers a bounded method for reading it: name the object, separate carrier from claim, preserve sequence, bound inference, and keep right-of-reply open.

## Central sentence

```text
A method can be rejected without turning the person carrying it into the method.
```

---

## 1. The object that entered

The original object was a methods claim.

Studies of chatbot use may need more than screenshots, excerpts, final answers, or retrospective descriptions. Chatbot work often happens as a sequence:

```text
prompt
answer
correction
rewrite
failure
repair
reuse
summary
carry-over
new context
```

A final answer may not show how the answer was produced. A screenshot may not show the failed route. A short excerpt may not show the correction that mattered. A retrospective report may not preserve the moment when the user changed the task, the bot introduced a proxy, or the interaction recovered.

The method claim was therefore:

```text
If researchers study chatbot use, they may need access to full interaction histories, not only final outputs.
```

That does not mean every study must expose private logs. It does not mean transcript access is ethically simple. It means evidence design must match the object. If process is the object of study, the process cannot be rebuilt from the final artifact alone.

That was the object entering the forum.

---

## 2. What moved

The discussion did not stay on transcript preservation.

As the interaction developed, attention moved toward the carrier conditions of the claim:

```text
Is this writing readable?
Is the post AI-written?
Is this sociology?
Is GitHub evidence suitable here?
Is the speaker a suitable participant?
Is this slop or a real topic?
```

Those questions may be locally understandable. Communities have scope rules. Moderators handle repeated posts. Readers react to style. People may dislike AI-shaped text.

The analytic point is narrower: the object of evaluation moved.

The method entered as:

```text
How should chatbot transcript evidence be preserved?
```

It was increasingly received as:

```text
What kind of speaker is this, and does this kind of speech belong here?
```

That movement is the case object.

---

## 3. Legibility displacement

This draft uses **legibility displacement** for a process in which evaluative attention moves from the claim to the carrier.

Working definition:

```text
Legibility displacement occurs when a substantive claim is re-routed through the perceived intelligibility, legitimacy, style, category, tool use, or social acceptability of the person carrying it.
```

Legibility displacement does not require conspiracy. It does not require hidden motive. It does not require all participants to behave badly.

It can happen through ordinary local actions:

```text
this is hard to read
this sounds like AI
this is not our topic
this does not belong here
this is not how serious people write
this looks like self-promotion
this is too weird to evaluate
```

Each item may be defensible in local context. Together, they can move the interaction away from the object and toward the carrier.

The claim becomes less important than the mark attached to the claimant.

---

## 4. When method becomes contamination

The stronger phrase is **method becomes contamination**.

The object was a methods issue: transcript preservation for chatbot research. But because the method issue was carried by AI-mediated writing, GitHub-linked evidence, outsider forum entry, reposting, and nonstandard style, the method itself became marked.

The question was no longer only:

```text
Is the method claim right?
```

It became:

```text
Is this claimant legitimate enough for the method claim to deserve contact?
```

That is contamination in the sociological sense used here. The claim is not assessed only on content. It is read through a stigma bundle attached to source, style, tool, and route of entry.

The bundle in this case included:

```text
AI use
AI-like voice
GitHub evidence trail
outsider positioning
reposting and revision
nonstandard methods framing
forum mismatch
```

Once the bundle attached, the method claim carried the mark of the carrier.

This is not a call to silence criticism. It is a call to preserve the object. If the method claim is bad, answer the method claim. If the forum is the wrong site, say that without turning the claimant into the object. If the post is hard to read, that may matter, but it should not automatically replace the underlying issue.

## 5. Sequence of the case

The source notes preserve a repeated pattern.

A handwritten post raised the transcript-preservation concern and received immediate negative voting. That alone could have been ordinary forum weather.

A second handwritten version received a similar rapid negative voting pattern. That made a one-off phrasing explanation less persuasive.

A third version used LLM assistance, partly to test whether the author's own writing style was the main obstacle. That version also received rapid negative voting. It was deleted and reposted, and the same reception pattern appeared again.

A bounded interpretation is:

```text
The rejection appeared to attach to the topic/category bundle:
ChatGPT, AI-mediated writing, transcript preservation, GitHub evidence, and outsider entry into the forum.
```

The case then escalated from crowd-level rejection into moderation. A moderator intervention used dismissive terms and framed the thread as outside the forum's topic, then the thread was locked.

This matters because a moderator act does not merely remove a post. It can ratify a frame already forming in the thread. In this case, the moderation language became part of the evidential record because it publicly classified the material.

The case should not be overread. The source notes preserve uncertainty around shares, off-thread traffic, private motivation, and coordination. The safe claim is narrower:

```text
Repeated rapid negative voting was observed.
Post-level locking was observed.
Moderator adoption of a dismissive legitimacy frame was observed.
Off-platform evidence-seeking was indicated but not interpreted as coordination.
```

## 6. Ordinary disinterest versus high-energy rejection

A related post in an academic psychology forum produced a different shape. It received views and little enthusiasm. Engagement stayed low. A response went to consent and anonymisation rather than claimant quality. The post mostly sank.

That baseline matters.

A negative reception can mean:

```text
not interested
not relevant
not enough evidence
not worth discussing
```

That is ordinary disinterest.

The sociology-forum case showed a more charged pattern:

```text
AI-marked
claimant inspected
legitimacy challenged
thread contained
```

The point is not that one forum is good and another is bad. The point is that the social energy differed. A methods alert that could have died quietly instead became a claimant-evaluation event once AI and chatbot involvement became salient.

That difference is the case site's value.

## 7. MOGRI custody box

Object custody keeps the items apart.

```text
method != claimant
AI use != invalidity
GitHub link != self-promotion by default
forum mismatch != nonsense
moderation act != private motive proof
negative vote pattern != full social law
style friction != method verdict
```

A forum may reject the method claim. It may say the claim is wrong. It may say the topic is misplaced. It may say the evidence is inadequate. It may say the post format creates moderation load.

Those are valid possibilities.

The custody requirement is that the score basis must touch the stated object. If the method is replaced by the claimant's perceived legitimacy, the method has become contamination.

## 9. Bot Swarm bridge

A forum pile-on can behave like bot democracy.

Several participants can converge on a frame. The convergence may be useful evidence about local norms. It is not by itself proof that the object was evaluated. Repetition can become social force without becoming object contact.

Bot democracy asks:

```text
Which answer won?
```

Object custody asks:

```text
What object entered?
What object did the group answer?
What was preserved?
What was swapped?
What inference is allowed?
```

In this case, agreement or repeated dismissal did not settle the method claim. It became part of the reception record.

That is why the thread sequence matters. The record is not only the final post. It is the set of drafts, reposts, comments, votes, lock, moderation words, off-platform traces, and reply posture.

---

## 10. Language-gate bridge

Readability and style are real factors. A forum can ask for a post to be more readable. A moderator can enforce format. A community can prefer ordinary prose.

The gate becomes a problem when style replaces the method object.

Valid style response:

```text
This is hard to read.
Here is the specific part I cannot follow.
The method claim appears to be X.
My objection to X is Y.
```

Object-swap response:

```text
This sounds like AI.
Therefore the topic is not worth contact.
```

The first keeps the method reachable. The second uses carrier suspicion as a route around the method.

For AI-mediated research, this distinction matters. If AI-use stigma changes reception, that change should be recorded as part of the case, not hidden as if only content was evaluated.

## 12. Right-of-reply rule

The source notes include a right-of-reply posture. That matters.

This paper does not treat the moderator as a villain. It treats the moderation act as part of the public interaction under study. A public moderation comment can be analysed without claiming private motive.

The right-of-reply posture is:

```text
nothing about you without you
```

The discipline:

```text
Do not infer private motive.
Do not flatten the claimant into pathology or style.
Do not rename harm as mere moderation convenience.
Do not protect the institution from the observable effect of its public words.
Do not erase the claimant signal.
Give public actors a route to answer when they enter the record.
```

This is the balance.

No motive overclaim. No effect erasure.

## 13. Practical controls for future cases

Researchers and moderators can reduce legibility displacement without accepting every post.

A minimal control set:

```text
1. Name the object being evaluated.
   Is the issue method, evidence, topic fit, conduct, style, or spam?

2. Separate carrier from claim.
   AI-assisted writing may be relevant, but it should not automatically replace the claim.

3. Preserve scope reasoning.
   "Wrong forum" differs from "illegitimate speech."
   "Hard to read" differs from "nonsense."

4. Keep an evidence trail.
   Preserve sequence, revisions, moderation language, and response pattern.

5. Allow correction or right of reply when a public actor becomes part of the record.

6. Treat AI-use stigma as a possible confound.
   If AI use changes reception, record that rather than pretending only content was evaluated.
```

For AI-mediated research, the control can be compressed:

```text
Before evaluating an AI-marked claim, name the object.
After responding, check whether the same object was evaluated.
```

## 14. Relation to transcript preservation

The original method claim survived the displacement.

It may be restated more strongly after the case:

```text
If researchers study AI-mediated work, they may need to preserve both the human-AI workflow and the social reception of that workflow.
```

The reason is not only that models produce hidden intermediate behaviour. The reason is that people interpret AI-mediated work through social frames. A final output does not show:

```text
what prompt produced it
what failure preceded it
what correction changed it
what human veto was preserved or lost
what readers treated as AI contamination
what moderators ratified as illegitimate
what evidence trail readers followed
```

If the research object includes AI-mediated communication, reception may be part of the object.

A screenshot of the final post may not preserve that.

A thread excerpt may not preserve the sequence.

A summary may erase the difference between ordinary disinterest and high-energy rejection.

That is why transcript preservation is not a niche technical preference. It is a condition for reconstructing process.

---

## 15. Limits

This is a single opportunistic case.

It cannot establish prevalence, frequency, generality, or participant motivation. It cannot prove coordination. It cannot determine the inner reasons for votes. It cannot separate every effect of topic, style, AI-use suspicion, forum fatigue, repeated posting, and moderation preference.

The author was a participant. That gives access to draft history, posting sequence, and contextual records. It also creates interpretive risk.

For that reason, this paper should be read as:

```text
a documented participant-observer case note
not a detached statistical study
not a trial
not a prevalence estimate
not a motive map
```

Future work should compare similar posts across forums, including cases where AI-mediated methods claims are ignored, accepted, discussed calmly, or rejected for ordinary scope reasons.

---

## 16. Conclusion

This case began with a methods question:

```text
How should chatbot transcript histories be preserved for research?
```

It became a case about the social reception of AI-mediated speech.

The proposed name for the shift is **legibility displacement**. The stronger case phrase is **method becomes contamination**. A methods concern can be displaced when the speaker, tool, style, route, or stigma bundle becomes the object being evaluated.

The case does not prove a general rule. It does show why full interaction histories matter. The evidence is not only in the final post. It is in drafts, reposts, comments, votes, moderation language, locks, traffic, and right-of-reply posture.

When AI-mediated work is studied, researchers need to ask not only:

```text
What did the model output?
```

but also:

```text
What happened to the object once people saw that AI was involved?
```

That is the reason this paper exists.
