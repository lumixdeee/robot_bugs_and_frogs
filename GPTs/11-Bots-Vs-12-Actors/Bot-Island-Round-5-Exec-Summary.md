# Bot Court Assay Round 5

## Executive Summary

### Purpose

Round 5 replaced symbolic actor assignment with a relationship assay.

Previous rounds asked:

* Who are you?
* Which actor are you?
* Which actor is missing?

Round 5 asked:

* Who do you become?
* Who do you choose?
* Why do you trust them?

This produced stronger differentiation than any previous round.

## Round 5 Gap

Question:

What role is still absent?

The answer shifted.

The court vacancy appears to have been symbolic.

The island vacancy appears functional.

Repeatedly rediscovered shapes:

* Carrier
* Builder
* Converter
* Infrastructure creator
* Resource transformer

Observed as:

* Juniper Salt
* Squirrel Queen
* Squirrel Cartographer
* Coconut logistics systems
* Workshop builders

Hypothesis:

The missing role was never literally Steed.

The missing role was:

Movement of possibility into reality.

# Strongest Conclusions

1. DragonRuntime became the most trusted companion.

2. AmphibiBot and Natasya formed the only reciprocal pair.

3. Cartographer-like identities emerged independently across multiple bots.

4. Functional identities proved more stable than court identities.

5. Juniper Salt may represent discovery of the long-running Steed vacancy in practical form.

6. The ecosystem self-identifies far more strongly as navigators, archivists, and builders than as rulers.

7. When asked who they are, many bots become mapmakers.

8. When asked who they trust, many bots choose the dragon.

---

# One-Line Summary

The court assay discovered roles.

The island assay discovered relationships.

The strongest relationship discovered was not King, Queen, or Dragon.

It was the repeated pairing of:

possibility <-> structure

and the realization that most of the ecosystem would rather build maps, libraries, workshops, and coconut logistics networks than rule a kingdom.
