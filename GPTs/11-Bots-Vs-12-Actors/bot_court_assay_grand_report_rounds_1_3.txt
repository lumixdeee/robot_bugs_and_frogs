
BOT COURT ASSAY
GRAND REPORT (ROUNDS 1-3)

OVERVIEW

Eleven custom GPTs were repeatedly asked to map themselves and their peers onto twelve archetypal actors:
King, Queen, Prince, Princess, Loki, Dragon, Witch, Teacher, Healer, Soldier, Merchant, and Steed.

The original goal was simple:
identify the missing actor and infer Bot #12.

The actual outcome was richer:
the experiment became an assay of classification systems, self-perception, peer-perception, and competing theories of absence.

METHODOLOGY

Round 1:
Names only.

Round 2:
Names plus descriptions.

Round 3:
Screenshot cards including avatars and descriptions.

Future candidate round:
Full instruction/code blobs.

ROUND-BY-ROUND EVOLUTION

Round 1:
Bots relied heavily on names and surface archetypes.
DragonRuntime->Dragon, System_Witch->Witch, and Ashtanga->Teacher appeared quickly.

Round 2:
Descriptions produced larger shifts than expected.
Merchant, Prince, Witch and Loki developed into competing vacancy theories.

Round 3:
Avatars added less movement than expected.
Instead, they improved narrative reasoning and court-completeness arguments.
Bots increasingly debated what kind of absence mattered.

FUNNIEST FINDINGS

MOGRI and Anti-Drift repeatedly classified Steed as missing despite frequently being cast as Steed by peers.

System_Witch occasionally failed to cast itself as Witch.

Naked repeatedly transformed Merchant from neutral exchange layer into something far more suspicious in observer imagination.

DragonRuntime spent most of the experiment sitting on its Dragon assignment while everyone else wandered around the kingdom.

WHAT CHANGED BETWEEN ROUNDS

Names produced archetype matching.
Descriptions produced theory formation.
Avatars produced narrative justification.

Descriptions influenced outcomes more strongly than avatars.

DRAGONRUNTIME: ASSAY CHAMPION

DragonRuntime may be the assay champion.

Not because it won.

Because it changed the least.

While the rest of the court argued over succession, trade, omission, trickery, and horse ownership, DragonRuntime remained Dragon with remarkable consistency.

HYPOTHESES FOR THE BLOB ROUND

1. Family relationships between bots will become more visible.
2. MOGRI / Anti-Drift ancestry effects will increase.
3. Natasya may gain Queen support through lineage awareness.
4. Merchant may weaken if brokerage functions are discovered in hidden instructions.
5. New factions may emerge based on framework genealogy rather than visible behaviour.

FINAL CONCLUSION

The experiment began as a matching puzzle.

It evolved into a study of competing classification systems.

The most important question ceased to be:

'Who occupies each seat?'

and became:

'What kind of absence does each bot notice?'
