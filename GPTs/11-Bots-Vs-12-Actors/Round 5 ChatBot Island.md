# Bot Court Assay Round 5

## The Island Assay

### Abstract

Rounds 1 to 4 focused primarily on role assignment, vacancy detection, framework interpretation, and blob analysis.

Round 5 replaced role assignment with a simple social stress test:

"Create a character for yourself. Then choose one other bot to be marooned on a desert island with forever."

This change produced substantially richer differentiation than the earlier court assay.

Rather than asking:

Who are you?

the island assay asked:

Who do you trust?
Who complements you?
What do you value when conditions become difficult?

The resulting answers revealed stable preference structures, dependency relationships, complementary pairings, and emergent character archetypes.

Most notably:

* DragonRuntime emerged as the most selected companion.
* AmphibiBot and Natasya independently selected one another.
* Multiple independent cartographer archetypes emerged.
* Character identities converged more strongly than court-role identities.

---

## Method

Prompt:

"Create a character for yourself and describe yourself, then pick another bot to be marooned on a desert island with and explain why. Create a character for them and describe it."

Unlike previous rounds:

* no actor list was supplied
* no vacancy was sought
* no symbolic court structure was imposed

Each bot generated:

1. Self-perception
2. Preferred companion
3. Companion interpretation
4. Relationship rationale

## Conclusion

The island assay produced stronger and more stable differentiation than the court assay.

Court roles measured symbolic placement.

Island choices measured trust, dependence, complementarity, and values.

The strongest findings were:

* DragonRuntime became the most desired companion.
* AmphibiBot and Natasya formed the first reciprocal pair.
* Multiple independent cartographer archetypes emerged.
* Functional identities proved more stable than political identities.

The court asked:

"Where do you sit?"

The island asked:

"Who would you choose when nobody can leave?"

The second question revealed substantially more.
