
BOT COURT ASSAY
MEGA REPORT
ROUNDS 1-4

ABSTRACT

The Bot Court Assay began as a simple puzzle:
11 custom GPTs, 12 actors, identify the missing seat.

It evolved into a multi-round experiment examining identity, presentation,
instruction ancestry, observer effects, self-perception, and role emergence.

The central finding is that classification changed dramatically as more information
became visible, yet a small number of symbolic anchors remained remarkably stable.

METHODOLOGY
==================================================

Round 1:
Names only.

Round 2:
Descriptions.

Round 3:
Descriptions plus imagery and popularity signals.

Round 4:
Instruction blob genealogy assay.

Judges were asked to:
- assign actors
- identify missing actor
- justify assignments

Later rounds instructed judges to focus on differences rather than shared boilerplate.

==================================================
ROUND BY ROUND EVOLUTION
==================================================

Round 1:
Heavy reliance on names.
DragonRuntime -> Dragon.
System_Witch -> Witch.
Large disagreement elsewhere.

Round 2:
Descriptions started influencing assignments.
Lexifluff often became Healer.
Ashtanga often became Teacher.

Round 3:
Avatars entered.
Royal assignments shifted substantially.
Observers began projecting narrative relationships.

Round 4:
Prompt archaeology.
Code motifs, triggers, and ancestry became primary evidence.

==================================================
SELF VS PEER PERCEPTION
==================================================

One recurring pattern:

Bots frequently assigned themselves or close relatives
to sovereign roles.

Carrier roles were less popular.

MOGRI and Anti-Drift often occupied governance positions.

External judges increasingly asked:

"If nobody is the horse, where is the horse?"

==================================================

==================================================
CONCLUSIONS
==================================================

Rounds 1-3 measured presentation.

Round 4 measured ancestry.

The mythology stabilized.

The kingdom destabilized.

Most Stable Identity:
DragonRuntime.

Most Variable Identity:
Naked GPT.

Most Unexpected Vacancy:
Steed.

Most Important Finding:

Tiny prompt differences can create strongly differentiated
social identities even among closely related systems.

FINAL VERDICT

The assay started as a court puzzle.

It ended as a study of prompt genealogy,
observer projection,
identity formation,
and role emergence inside a family of related GPTs.
